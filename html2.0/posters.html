DotGNU Posters for Free Software Advocacy

The goal of the DotGNU Posters Project is to create a cool poster which will promote the Free Software movement in general and at the same time also raise awareness of the DotGNU project. We would like DotGNU to be seen in the context of the overall desire of the Free Software movement to provide businesses with viable alternatives to proprietary software for all business processes. One major challenge in this area is to provide a migration path which provides significant benefits already during the first stages of the transition to Free Software. DotGNU helps here, because the webservices approach allows to make the transition in multiple small steps.

The goal is to get a dozen of these posters put up in every computer science department, of universities and technical colleges worldwide. This requires some funding for printing of the posters and postage, and while working on securing the needed financial resources we may also succeed in obtaining some prizes so that we can hold a DotGNU posters competion.

How to contribute

Please use GIMP, the GNU Image Manipulation Program for creating graphics files for this project, and put them under the GNU Free Documentation License.

An anonymous ftp area has been set up on dotgnu.org where you can upload poster ideas, drafts, submissions, etc. to the incoming directory. After uploading something, please let us know by sending email to posters@dotgnu.org

Maybe a New Face for DotGNU?

With the upcoming 0.5.0 release of pnet, and a new year beginning, it may also be a good time to redress DotGNU in general. So besides contributions towards the DotGNU Poster, we are also interested in new slogan and logo ideas.

Slogan

While our current slogan of Making The Web Work For You is quite ok, we would like to see what other people can come up with. So if you have some witty verbiage you'd like to spew out, let us know, and you may get to see your words plastered all over the world.

Logo

Same story with the logo. We think the one we have rocks, but I am also excited to see what other people can come up with. So please, if you're talented in this sort of thing, help us out.

 

Distribution and Fund Raising Organizer

We need someone to organize distribution and the funds for poster printing. If you have experience in this area and would like us to be forever indebted to you, please contact us.

Further Plans and Ideas

  • It should be possible to find at every campus at least volunteer who cares enough about Free Software to help us out by making sure that the posters are put up in good places.
  • However, producing the posters will require funding. We need a significant number of posters to reach all computer science departments (how many are there, worldwide, anyway?). Postage will be a significant expense too.
  • How do we get funding for this? Maybe it is best to approach companies which are not in the software business concerning this.
  • Since this project needs funding anyway, we can try to get some additional funding so that we can hold a competition and have some nice prizes for those who contributed significantly to what is in the end chosen as DotGNU poster.
