DotGNU Posters Project

The goal of this project is to create a cool high-quality poster for DotGNU, which emphasizes the freedom aspects of Free software in some way, and to make sure that these posters are put up in highly-visible places in every computer science department of universities and technical colleges worldwide.

Please use GIMP, the GNU Image Manipulation Program for creating graphics files for this project, and put them under the GNU Free Documentation License.

The coordinating committee of the DotGNU posters project currently consists of Ian Fung (irc nick: alias) and Norbert Bollow (irc nick: nb). Both can often be found on the irc channel #dotgnu at irc.freenode.net and they can also be reached via the email address posters@dotgnu.org

Wanted: Advice from someone who has experience with this kind of thing

Who do you know who can give us advice on how to proceed with this project? Please put us in touch with them!

Current ideas

  • It should be possible to find at every campus at least volunteer who cares enough about Free Software to help us out by making sure that the posters are put up in good places.
  • However, producing the posters will require funding. We need a significant number of posters to reach all computer science departments (how many are there, worldwide, anyway?). Postage will be a significant expense too.
  • How do we get funding for this? Maybe it is best to approach companies which are not in the software business concerning this.
  • Since this project needs funding anyway, we could try to get some additional funding so that we can hold a competition and have some nice prizes for those who contributed significantly to what is in the end chosen as DotGNU poster.
